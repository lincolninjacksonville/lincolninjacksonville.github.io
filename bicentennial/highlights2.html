Programs in Jacksonville will examine visual images of Lincoln

January 19, 2009 — The image of Abraham Lincoln through the camera lens will form the basis for two presentations in Jacksonville Monday, January 26, by a DePaul University scholar who has studied how photography shaped the public's perception of the 16 th president.

Mark Pohlad, associate professor of art history at DePaul, will present “The Camera and Abraham Lincoln” at 11 a.m. in Rammelkamp Chapel on the campus of Illinois College. His second program, “Abraham Lincoln in American Art,” is set for 7 p.m. at Jacksonville's David Strawn Art Gallery.

The programs, which are free and open to the public, will be held under the auspices of the Jacksonville Lincoln Bicentennial Celebration Committee. Pohlad specializes in American art and the history of photography, and he holds a doctorate from the University of Delaware.

His first presentation will describe the photographs of Lincoln as they appear throughout the history of photography, according to James Marshall, associate dean of the college.

“Lincoln was the first extensively photographed president and the first for whom the media helped sway an election,” Dr. Marshall remarked. Pohlad's program will trace the images of Lincoln while describing the nature and the challenges of photography in the mid 19 th century.

Dr. Marshall also said the program will provide a lesson on the early years of photography in America while providing a visual synopsis of the life of Abraham Lincoln.
